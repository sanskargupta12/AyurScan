MEDICINAL PLANTS

Tulsi
(Holy Basil)

Tulsi is known for its immunity-boosting and antibacterial properties. It is used for treating coughs, colds, and respiratory ailments. It also helps in reducing stress and promoting overall well-being.

Ayurvedic

Neem

Neem leaves have antibacterial, antifungal, and blood-purifying properties. They are used for skin conditions like acne, as well as for dental health and digestion.

Ayurvedic

Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera leaves are known for their soothing and healing properties. They are used for skin conditions, burns, and to promote digestive health.

Ayurvedic

Curry Leaves

Curry leaves are used in Ayurveda for their digestive and carminative properties. They help in managing indigestion and diarrhea.

Ayurvedic

Ashwagandha

Although the root is more commonly used, Ashwagandha leaves also have medicinal value. They are used to reduce stress, improve cognitive function, and enhance vitality.

Ayurvedic

Brahmi

Brahmi leaves are used for their memory-enhancing and cognitive-boosting properties. They are often used in Ayurvedic preparations for mental clarity.

Ayurvedic

Moringa

Moringa leaves are rich in vitamins and minerals. They are used for their nutritional value and are believed to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Ayurvedic

Guduchi
(Giloy)

Guduchi leaves are used to boost the immune system and for their anti-inflammatory properties. They are often used in Ayurvedic remedies for fever and respiratory issues.

Ayurvedic

Bilva
(Bael)

Bilva leaves are used in Ayurveda for their digestive benefits. They are known to relieve digestive problems like diarrhea and dysentery.
